> [!Note]
> 
> If we were to be **theoretically pure** in the sense that no two grammar categories that are 100% identical in morphology are represented, we would go through and merged them, erasing non-identifying inflection values when appropriate.
> 
> However, there's a cost: the tables would then feel tangibly incomplete. The inflection patterns of Spanish are so strong and would be so close to complete, that when a hole appears it's felt. The reader might wonder what form of *hablar* is used for a certain grammatical category, since it wouldn't be listed.
> 
> Technically the author of the dictionary could enter one form and re-use it in multiple rows in the inflection tables, but this is discouraged in the Tollerus UI (and an unsupported outcome of some data filling features). I think instead, it's actually worth some minimal conceded duplication with the following explicit list of forms:
> * "hablamos" (first person plural, present vs. preterite)
> * "hablaba" (1st vs. 3rd person singular imperfect indicative)
> * "hablaría" (1st vs. 3rd person singular conditional)
> * "hable" (1st vs. 3rd person singular present subjunctive)
> * "hablara" (1st vs. 3rd person singular imperfect subjunctive)
> * "hablare" (1st vs. 3rd person singular future subjunctive)
> * the 3 repurposed imperative forms "habla"/"hable"/"hablen"
